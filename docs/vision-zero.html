Data & methods

What's shown

Traffic deaths and serious injuries on surface streets across the City of Houston, 2016–2025 plus a partial 2026. Source: TxDOT CRIS public crash extracts.

Which roads

We exclude limited-access freeways, tollways, ramps and frontage roads (a different facility type, and their crashes snap onto nearby streets), but keep at-grade arterials regardless of owner, including state-owned ones like S Main / US-90A, SH 6, and Westheimer / FM 1093. This matches Austin's Vision Zero dashboard and the City's own High Injury Network, which include state arterials.

Road ownership

Each street and crash is labeled city-owned vs TxDOT-owned (from TxDOT's roadway inventory) so the dashboard can show the state-owned share; the City must partner with TxDOT to redesign those. A street is counted as TxDOT-owned only where it runs parallel to a state roadway, so a city street that merely bridges over a freeway is not mislabeled.

How crashes map to streets

Each crash is assigned to its nearest street within 200 ft (median 4 ft).

Definitions

Killed or seriously injured (KSI) = the K + A levels on the KABCO injury scale.

Years of life lost = years before age 75 summed over those killed (CDC YPLL convention). The public extract records a victim age for only about half of fatalities, so the rest are estimated at the average, making the total an estimate.

Neighborhood income = the median household income of the Census block group around each crash (an area characteristic, not the individual's).

Counts, not rates

Streets are ranked and shaded by the number of people killed or seriously injured, not a rate per traffic volume. This is the Vision Zero convention (and how the City's own High Injury Network is built): the goal is zero deaths, so attention goes where the most harm actually occurs. Counts are not normalized for exposure (vehicle miles traveled or foot traffic), which we lack for most local streets.

Data sources

Every dataset here is public. Each link goes to the official source.
